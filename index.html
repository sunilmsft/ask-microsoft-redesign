Option E: AI-Native Assistant

Modern guided experience

  • Welcome: Task-based entry cards — zero text wall
  • Chips: Contextual follow-ups after each response
  • Human Offramp: Seamless inline handoff after 2 exchanges
  • Stay-In: Inline action buttons (no redirect feel)

What We Can Learn — Without Copying

Insights adapted for the Microsoft brand and Ask Microsoft's unique position as a sales-assist chat.

▲
From Intercom: Human handoff visibility
Their "Talk to a person" is always visible. Our callback/phone icons are good but could be more discoverable. Consider a text label on hover or first-visit tooltip.
▲
From Zendesk: Privacy-first framing
They lead with privacy before the greeting. We don't need to go that far, but our Transparency FAQ + opt-out approach is actually stronger for compliance.
▲
From Salesforce: Ultra-minimal greeting
"Ask Agentforce anything" — brave simplicity. Our D option is close at ~18 words. We could test an even shorter variant: "Hi, I'm here to help with M365."
▲
From Tidio/Industry: Action-verb chips
Best-in-class chips use action verbs: "Track my order", "Find my plan." Consider "Which plan fits me?" vs generic "Compare plans."
★
Our Edge: Sales-assist context
Most competitors are support-only. Ask Microsoft is a sales + support hybrid. The smart prompts and plan comparison KB give us a unique advantage none of them have.
★
Our Edge: Fluent 2 design system
No competitor has a design system as mature as Fluent 2 behind their chat. Our visual consistency, accessibility, and brand coherence are market-leading.
▼
Gap: No proactive engagement
Intercom and Zendesk offer page-context triggers (e.g., show pricing help on the pricing page). Ask Microsoft could trigger contextual chips based on the page a customer is browsing.
▼
Gap: No post-conversation follow-up
Zendesk sends CSAT surveys and Intercom tracks resolution quality. Ask Microsoft could add a brief "Was this helpful?" flow after a conversation ends.
Bottom line: Ask Microsoft is already competitive on greeting UX, trust/transparency, and visual design. The biggest opportunities are in (1) making human-assist options more discoverable, (2) testing shorter greetings, (3) page-context-aware chip suggestions, and (4) post-conversation feedback loops. Our unique sales-assist positioning and Fluent 2 foundation are genuine differentiators.
